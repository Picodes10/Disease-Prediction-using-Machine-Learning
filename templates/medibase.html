Heart Disease
Definition:
  • Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart
Symptoms:
  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
Causes:
  • High blood pressure
  • Cholesterol
  • Smoking
Prevention:
  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Manage stress
Diagnosis:
  • ECG
  • Blood tests
  • Chest X-ray
  • Coronary angiogram
Treatment:
  • Medications (beta blockers, statins)
  • Lifestyle changes
  • Angioplasty or bypass surgery
Risk Factors:
  • Age
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Obesity
  • Family history
Diabetes
Definition:
  • Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body is unable to properly process blood glucose
Symptoms:
  • Frequent urination
  • Excessive thirst
  • Increased hunger
  • Blurred vision
Causes:
  • Genetics
  • Obesity
  • Poor lifestyle
  • Insulin resistance
Prevention:
  • Healthy diet
  • Exercise
  • Maintain ideal weight
  • Regular checkups
Diagnosis:
  • Fasting blood sugar test
  • A1C test
  • Glucose tolerance test
Treatment:
  • Insulin therapy
  • Oral medications
  • Dietary control
  • Exercise
Risk Factors:
  • Family history
  • Inactivity
  • Overweight
  • High blood pressure
Liver Disease
Definition:
  • Liver disease encompasses any condition that damages the liver and affects its function
Symptoms:
  • Jaundice
  • Fatigue
  • Dark urine
  • Swelling in the abdomen
Causes:
  • Hepatitis viruses
  • Alcohol abuse
  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease
Prevention:
  • Avoid alcohol and drug abuse
  • Vaccination
  • Maintain healthy weight
Diagnosis:
  • Blood tests
  • Imaging (ultrasound/CT)
  • Liver biopsy
Treatment:
  • Medication
  • Lifestyle changes
  • Liver transplant in severe cases
Risk Factors:
  • Alcohol use
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Unsafe needle practices

Anemia
Definition:
  • Anemia is a condition where the body lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin, reducing oxygen transport to tissues.
Symptoms:
  • Fatigue
  • Pale skin
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold hands and feet
Causes:
  • Iron deficiency
  • Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency
  • Chronic diseases
  • Blood loss (e.g., menstruation, injury)
  • Genetic disorders (e.g., sickle cell)
Prevention:
  • Iron-rich diet (leafy greens, red meat)
  • Vitamin supplements when needed
  • Regular screenings in at-risk individuals
  • Treat underlying conditions
Diagnosis:
  • Complete blood count (CBC)
  • Iron studies
  • Vitamin B12/folate test
  • Bone marrow biopsy (rare cases)
Treatment:
  • Iron or vitamin supplements
  • Blood transfusion (in severe cases)
  • Treating the underlying cause
  • Dietary changes
Risk Factors:
  • Poor diet
  • Menstruation
  • Pregnancy
  • Chronic illnesses
  • Family history of anemia
Asthma
Definition:
  • Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition where airways become inflamed and narrowed, causing difficulty in breathing.
Symptoms:
  • Wheezing
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest tightness
  • Coughing, especially at night or early morning
Causes:
  • Allergens (pollen, dust mites, mold)
  • Air pollution
  • Respiratory infections
  • Physical activity (exercise-induced)
Prevention:
  • Avoid known triggers
  • Maintain clean indoor air
  • Get vaccinated against flu and pneumonia
  • Use controller medications as prescribed
Diagnosis:
  • Spirometry
  • Peak flow test
  • Methacholine challenge
  • Allergy testing
Treatment:
  • Inhaled corticosteroids
  • Bronchodilators (rescue inhalers)
  • Leukotriene modifiers
  • Allergy shots (immunotherapy)
Risk Factors:
  • Family history of asthma
  • Allergies
  • Smoking or secondhand smoke
  • Obesity
  • Exposure to pollutants
PCOS
Definition:
  • PCOS is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age, involving irregular periods, excess androgen, and polycystic ovaries.
Symptoms:
  • Irregular or missed periods
  • Excess facial or body hair
  • Acne or oily skin
  • Weight gain
  • Thinning hair on the scalp
Causes:
  • Hormonal imbalance (e.g., high androgens)
  • Insulin resistance
  • Genetic factors
  • Low-grade inflammation
Prevention:
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Balanced diet
  • Regular physical activity
  • Monitor blood sugar levels
Diagnosis:
  • Pelvic ultrasound
  • Blood tests (androgens, insulin, etc.)
  • Physical exam
  • Medical history review
Treatment:
  • Birth control pills to regulate hormones
  • Anti-androgen medications
  • Metformin for insulin resistance
  • Lifestyle modifications
Risk Factors:
  • Family history of PCOS
  • Obesity
  • Insulin resistance
  • Sedentary lifestyle
